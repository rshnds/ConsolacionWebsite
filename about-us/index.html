MUNICIPALITY OF CONSOLACION

The Gateway to the North

Nestled between the vibrant urban hubs of Cebu City and Mandaue, the Municipality of Consolacion offers a serene retreat just minutes away from the bustling metropolis.

From Kampi-ig to Consolacion

Once a quiet barrio called Kampi-ig, Consolacion has grown into a bustling municipality with a story of resilience.

Originally part of Mandaue, it became an independent town in 1871, only to face challenges that led to its reintegration with Mandaue in the early 1900s.

Through determination, the people petitioned for independence, and in 1920, Consolacion was reborn as a municipality. It was named in honor of the governor’s daughter.

Cultural Pride

Every February 14, Consolacion bursts into life with the Sarok Festival, a vibrant homage to its agricultural roots. The festival celebrates the Sarok, an eco-friendly bamboo-and-banana-leaf hat once worn by farmers to shield themselves. From colorful street dancing to intricately designed Sarok hats, the festival is a testament to the town’s creative spirit and cultural heritage.

Consolacion is also home to the Miligoy de Cebu, a traditional courtship dance performed with bamboo castanets.

Nature and Leisure

Consolacion’s rolling hills and fertile landscapes are home to mountain cafes, eco-farms, and scenic spots perfect for relaxation.

Recent Achievements

Consolacion’s commitment to resilience and excellence was recognized with the prestigious Gawad KALASAG award, honoring its disaster risk management and humanitarian efforts. This achievement reflects the community's dedication to safety and progress.
November 26, 2024

Consolacion's dedication to good governance and public service excellence was celebrated with the prestigious 2024 Seal of Good Local Governance for Region VII. This recognition highlights the collective efforts of Mayor Teresa P. Alegado, local officials, department heads, and LGU staff in fostering transparency, accountability, and community progress.
